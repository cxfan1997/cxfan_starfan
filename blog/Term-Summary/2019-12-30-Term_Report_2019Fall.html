Fall 2019 Term Report

Posted on 2019-12-30 Edited on 2021-09-20 In Term Summary

摘要

2019年秋季学期圆满画上句号。《在美留学手册》中，五年留学生活中的第一年被称为是“适应期”。远赴重洋来到安娜堡小城，除了适应异国他乡的生活，还要找到更加高效的学习工作方法，来适应西式的研究生学习风格和节奏。在第一学期，Chongxing同学在保持身心健康，坚持体育锻炼，丰富课下生活的前提下，明确了日常工作中的“三座大山”：课程学习、科研工作和英语提升，并且在这些方面做出了非常好的成绩。虽然在此过程中仍然暴露出很多问题，不过对于刚刚入学的新生来说，这样的成绩已经可以打到85分了。

学期目标

  • 适应安娜堡的环境，为五年留学生活开个好头
  • 聚焦专业课程的学习，适当参与研究小组的工作
  • 大幅度提高自己的英语能力

课程学习

综述

表现突出的方面

  • 延续了本科期间的学习态度和学习习惯，状态良好
  • 坚持高效的学习方法，利用知识网络图进行梳理
  • 与各科老师保持了良好的沟通，进行了多次一对一谈话，收获很大

仍待改进的方面

  • 课堂上的表现积极性仍然欠缺

本学期一共选修五门课程，其中551为专业核心课（知识），473为专业选修课（由老师推荐），747和749为专业核心课（技能），531为跨专业选修课。

学习态度和学习习惯方面，总的来说保持了本科期间的认真专注，对每一科基本上都能认真对待。上课听讲基本上认真对待，除551课程后半部分的“不屑一顾”和749课程上的“上A课做B事”（可接受范围），唯一的不足在于上课期间的活跃度低，尤其是对于749需要通过互动来达到更好的学习效果的课程来说，这一点非常重要。各科作业认真完成，高效高质，无缺交作业，1次迟交作业（非Canvas系统追踪的作业），成绩评价大多在90%以上，极个别在85%左右。

学习方法方面，基本延续了“预习-学习-复习”三步走方法，整体而言保证质量的前提下，学习效率位于平均以上。总体来看学习和复习两个环节做的非常好，尤其是复习阶段的知识网络梳理，这使得Chongxing对于每个章节的重要内容都有了更加宏观的认识和更深刻的理解，目前制作知识网络梳理使用的是MindMaster，质量和效率都很上乘，推荐后期继续使用。预习阶段有些乏力，一部分原因在于授课老师的课件发放时间较晚，无法完成预习工作。如果要提一些锦上添花的建议，如果能够对知识型课程（例如473、551）进行一些额外的课外阅读和课外思考，效果可以更好（不过考虑到目前的时间安排，这一点实现起来或许不太现实）。

CLIMATE 551 – Advanced Fluid Mechanics (进阶流体动力学)

学习内容

目标（Learning Objectives）

  1. Be able to explain the conditions when groups of particles can be treated as a “fluid” and be able to describe conditions when the fluid approximation breaks down and particles cannot be treated as a fluid.
  2. Be able to apply dimensional analysis to problems to make order of magnitude estimates, simplify differential equations and determine units of quantities, including integrals of distributions functions.
  3. Be able to use dimensional analysis to determine geometrically and dynamically similar flow regimes and the non-dimensional numbers that govern these types of regimes. Examples considered in class will include the Reynolds and Rossby numbers, but students should be able to apply dimensional analysis to new problems and situations.
  4. Be able to write down and provide a physical explanation for each of the terms in the equations describing conservation of mass and linear-momentum for a fluid.
  5. Be able to explain the difference between Eulerian and Lagrangian reference frames and be able to write down equations that transform between reference frames.
  6. Be able to linearize differential equations and use the linearized equations to obtain dispersion relations and/or growth rates of instabilities. Examples considered in class may vary, but could include sound waves, gravity waves, Rossby waves along with Rayleigh-Taylor instability, Kelvin-Helmholtz instability. Students should be able to apply the linearization procedure to new problems beyond those covered in class.
  7. Be able to explain, using words and equations, the difference between stable and unstable flow.
  8. Be able to explain, using words and equations, the difference between the phase and group speed of waves.
  9. Be able to write down equations describing the Coriolis and Centrifugal forces and explain the effect of these forces using mathematical and physical arguments.

CLIMATE 473 – Climate Physics (气候物理学)

学习内容

目标（Learning Objectives）

  1. Understand the components of the energy budget at the surface and top-of-atmosphere, how they vary in space and time, and how they relate to equilibrium planetary emission temperature
  2. Represent and calculate climate sensitivity using a linear framework of independent feedback mechanisms
  3. Understand Earth's key physical climate feedback mechanisms, including temperature (Stefan-Boltzmann), water vapor, lapse rate, albedo, and cloud feedbacks
  4. Describe how different types of clouds and cloud properties influence Earth's terrestrial and solar energy budgets
  5. Apply an idealized mathematical framework to describe the transient response of global mean surface temperature to time-varying radiative forcings

CLIMATE 747 – Student Seminar (提案写作)

学习内容

目标

The goal of this course is to help students to develop the necessary skills to write a successful proposal to a US funding agency, and to deliver a successful talk to a conference on their research topic. Students will select, together with their Ph.D. advisor, a research topic they would like to propose to NASA’s Future Investigators in NASA Earth and Space Science and Technology (FINESST) program. Each section of the proposal text will be built, with comments from the instructor as well as from a Peer Review Team. The final product of this course will be a full, submittable proposal, made of the sections developed during the course, which will be evaluated by the instructor. This proposal will be presented by each student to the class during a talk at the end of the course, whose duration will depend on schedule and class size, and will be announced during the course.

结构

  1. Writing a proposal
    • Abstract
    • Introduction (challenging)
    • Scientific Questions (key)
    • Relevance to NASA
    • Methodology
    • Expected Results
    • Timeline and Milestones
  2. Lectures - The life for a scientist
    • Writing successful proposal
    • The proposal in scientist life
    • The proposal journey
    • The talk in scientist life

ELI 531 – Language & Communication I (口语对话)

学习内容

目标

  • Increase your fluency, comfort, and confidence communicating in one-on-one and small group interactions:
    • Elicit information
    • Express and elaborate ideas and opinions
    • Respond to opposing viewpoints
    • Clarify misunderstandings
    • Initiate and sustain interactions
    • Understand and negotiate speaker roles in various formal + informal interactions
    • Listen effectively in interactive contexts
  • Write appropriate academic emails
  • Interact effectively with professors and advisors in office hours
  • Take an active role in presentations and discussions
  • Interact effectively in social conversation

结构

  1. Discourse management
    • Phrases to accomplish conversational moves
    • Active listening (backchannel) signals
    • Conversational fillers to provide time to think
    • Discourse markers to aid paraphrasing & confirmation moves in conversation
    • Discourse markers to support clarification, hesitation, thinking time, redirection in conversation
    • Starting a conversation
    • Ending a conversation
  2. Formality & politeness
    • Using hedging as a conversational politeness move
    • Language for polite academic asymmetric interactions
    • Shifting registers in conversation
    • Language for polite social interactions & requests
  3. Questions
    • (Polite) question formulation
    • Information-question formulation
    • Confirmation/paraphrasing strategies, formulating clarification questions
  4. Discussions
    • Formulating open-ended discussion questions
    • Paraphrasing others' contributions in a discussion
    • Hedging arguments in a discussion
    • Phrases for polite agreement, disagreement, redirection, clarification (Imber's Verbal Stratagems)
  5. Naming
    • Pronunciation of proper names (esp. buildings & street names)
    • Terms of address (Mr., Ms., Professor, Dr., etc.)
  6. Vocabulary
    • Using corpora to find setting-specific wording (e.g. yohasebe.com/tcse, english-corpora.org/tv)
  7. Fluency
    • Using circumlocution
    • Signaling new content through intonation
    • Scripting, rehearsing, impromptu role playing for fluency development
  8. E-communication
    • Email opening & closing language
    • Email organizational patterns
    • Purpose and audience-dependent register in emails and online discussions

科研项目

Solar Farming Project

项目目标

  1. Quantify the change in surface reflectance, emissivity, and LST from satellite observation.
    • Spatial map: draw the contour maps to see whether there was a significant albedo/emissivity change over the solar farm compared to the surrounding regions.
    • Time series: draw the time series of the solar farm and other surrounding regions to see whether the transition happened exactly during the installation of solar farms.
    • Difference bar charts: draw the difference before and after the installation of solar panels across all spectral bands as a bar chart.
  2. Calculate the broadband flux from a radiative transfer model to get a real sense of radiation budget change
    • ModTran model with two parameter schemes: Barren 56 or MODIS observed.
  3. Draw the global reflectance maps to see how much area on Earth could be covered with solar panels and seen such an effect in our case study.

项目过程

  • 阅读了相关的文献，主要是两篇模式研究的文章，了解了研究背景
  • 对卫星数据进行了下载
  • 使用NCL编程语言，对下载的数据进行处理，绘制图像

项目成果

  1. Some interesting findings
    • A significant albedo decrease is observed only over the solar farms and only during the installation of solar panels across all bands.
    • A slight change of emissivity is observed over the solar farm and during the installation of solar panels.
    • RTM suggests that upward flux decreases by up to 23% at surface and 18% at TOA.
    • Global surface reflectance contour shows the area that can be covered with solar panels and seen such an effect in our case analysis.
  2. Integrated with CLIMATE 747, a complete proposal is written (not publishable here now).
  3. Integrated with CLIMATE 473, a complete report of my work in the first term is written (not publishable here now).

后期展望

  • 对第一阶段的工作做进一步的优化和完善，深度思考
