Posted on 2019-10-14 Edited on 2019-10-22 In English

Systematic English Self-Study Plan

Now it is enough for me to survive in University of Michigan based on the English skills I have now. However, coursework and research this term reflect a strong demand on strengthening English learning. This plan is drafted in order to improve my English skills systematically and efficiently.

Purpose

  • Develop good habits and consciously train my English proficiency.
  • Be able to speak confidently (not fluently since it is difficult to reach in the short term) in an informal setting.

Plan Details

Four components of English training and their corresponding plans are as followed:

  1. Listening
    • Daily Local News. Watch CBS news every evening during 7 – 8 pm without subtitles. Writing a summary of the news today is recommended but not required for now. It is a good idea to share what you learned from the news this week in writing section or speaking section.
    • Podcasts. Choose 3-5 podcasts with the app Google Podcasts and listen to one casually when doing something physical, at 80% speed. Writing a summary is recommended but not required for now. It is a good idea to share what you learned from these podcasts this week in writing section or speaking section. This is required when jogging on the trails or on the treadmill every week (twice a week, Tue & Sat).
    • Purposed Listening Practice. Choose a story at npr.org with a transcript and plug the transcript into Vocab Grabber before listening. Look up some difficult words in the dictionary before listening to the audio without looking at the transcript. Writing a summary of the story is recommended but not required for now. It is a good idea to share what you learned from some interesting stories this week in writing section or speaking section. This is required at least twice a week (Mon & Thurs).
  2. Reading
    • Science Magazine Reading. Read certain portions of each issue every week. It is required that “EDITORIAL”, at least one article in “PERSPECTIVES” and at least one article in other sections should be read every week (Tue, Fri & Sun). Before reading, copy and paste the material into Vocab Grabber, part of Visual Thesaurus to explore the words in it before reading. Write a short paragraph of summary in the daily email to friends (see the writing section below).
  3. Writing
    • Daily Email in a Group. Invite anyone who is willing to do this training into a group (with Pamela and Shaina). Write a daily email to the group to share the stories, the research progress and anything you want to with group members. Pay attention to the special instructions from other sections.
    • Annotated Bibliography. Read at least one research paper (scheduled by yourself, not assigned by course lecturer or your advisor. It would be better to read a review) in your field once every week (Thurs). Write a summary and a few comments. It may include how/why this is useful to you, how it connects with other published research and what’s problematic about it. You can synthesize it with your mind map when you read your paper.
  4. Speaking
    • ELI 531 in this Fall 2019 term (Tue).
    • ELI Conversation Circle in this Fall 2019 term (Sun).
    • Weekly Video. Install the free app Box Capture on your iPad, use your UMich free umich.box.com account to set up a shared folder with Pamela and record videos that will automatically upload to this folder once a week (Fri) - maybe research updates, maybe stories from the week. Video length is suggested to be about 10 minutes. Advanced preparation is accepted in the first phase, but it would be better to deliver an impromptu talk later in the training.

The execution of the plans will be supervised by ELI 531 course lecturer Pamela. Weekly progress report should be delivered to the supervisor, no matter how you did last week. Further modification of the plans should be expected if the outcome is not satisfactory.
