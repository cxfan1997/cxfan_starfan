Satellite Observation of Aerosol-Precipitation Relationship

Chongxing Fan, Minghuai Wang
Sun, Jun 30, 2019 Projects

Abstract

The adjustment of cloud amount to aerosol effects occurs to a large extent in response to the aerosol effect on precipitation. This is studied here for marine boundary layer water clouds by analyzing the dependence of rain intensity, as measured by the radar onboard the Global Precipitation Measurement (GPM), on cloud properties as measured by MODIS. Here we show that detectable rain initiates when the drop effective radius at the cloud top exceeds 14 $\mu$m, and precipitation is strongly suppressed with increasing cloud drop concentration ($N_d$), which contributes to the strong dependence of cloud amount on aerosols. This dependence of precipitation on $N_d$ and cloud geometric thickness presents a simple framework for precipitation susceptibility to aerosols, which explains other kinds of previously observed relationships. Our results further show that sorting data by cloud geometrical thickness and using alternative CCN proxy rather than aerosol optical depth (AOD) are critical for studying aerosol-cloud-precipitation interactions.
